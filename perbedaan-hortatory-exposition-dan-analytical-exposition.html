9. what is the future of explanation text, procedure text, analytical exposition dan hortatory exposition?​


Explanatory text is a text that explains the process of occurrence or formation of a natural phenomenon or social event. In explanatory text, an event occurs because there is an incident another before, and that event will result in another event after that.

Procedure text is a text that presents the steps of activities or various stages to do or produce something.

Analytical Exposition is a text that tries to provide a comprehensive or thorough explanation of a problem by carefully presenting supporting opinions.

Hortatory Exposition is a type of persuasive text or oral material, often used to explain whether something should not be done.

13. Please you write down different between analytical exposition and hortatory exposition 1 Tuliskan defition 2 Tuliskan generik struktur dari keduanya​


Jawaban:

Reading Expository text

Analytical exposition dan Hortatory exposition merupakan jenis teks bahasa Inggris yang sangat identik.

• Analytical Exposition merupakan teks yang digunakan penulis untuk menjelaskan tenatang suatu isu agar para pembaca sadar akan isu tersebut.

• Hortatory Exposition merupakan teks yang bersifat persuasif (mengajak) untuk melakukan ataupun untuk tidak melakukan suatu hal.

Perbedaannya terletak pada fungsi teks, jika analytical exposition berfungsi untuk menyadarkan pembaca akan suatu isu, hortatory exposition lebih menekankan pada pembaca untuk setuju dan mengikuti apa yang dijelaskan pada teks.

Penjelasan:

=> Generic structure dan ciri - ciri teks :

1. Analytical Exposition

• generic structure :

Title [judul]Thesis [pendapat penulis terkait topik]Arguments [pendapat yang mendukung, alasan mengapa topik itu diangkat]Conclusion [kesimpulan atau pernyataan ulang terkait topik]

• ciri - ciri :

Berisi argumen yang disertai alasan pendukung.Menggunakan Simple Present tenseMenggunakan kata penghubung (first, second, next)Menggunakan causal conjuntion (hubungan sebab akibat) [because, for that reason]

2. Hortatory Exposition

• generic structure :

Title [judul]Thesis [topik pembicaraan]Arguments [pendapat yang mendukung]Recomendation [saran terkait topik pembicaraan]

Berbeda dengan Analytical exposition yang diakhiri dengan conclusion, Hortatory exposition lebih menekankan dengan saran diakhir teksnya.

• ciri - ciri

Teks persuasif (banyak kalimat ajakan)Menggunakan simple present tenseMenggunakan thinking verb (I think, I believe)Menggunakan temporal connective (firstly, secondly)Menggunakan kalimat rekomendasi (in my opinion, it is better to, you should)

26. 1. PERBEDAAN DARI HORTATORY EXPOSITION VERSUS ANALYTICAL EXPOSITION VERSUS DISCUSS


Hortatory exposition is a type of spoken or written text that is intended to explain the listeners or reader that something should or should not happen, atau dengan kata lain hortatory text merupakan bentuk text yang berfungsi untuk menjelaskan akan suatu hal dan berfungsi untuk mempengaruhi pembaca atau pendengar nya.

INGGRIS:

1.Analytical exposition is a type of text that presents an argument and provides evidence to support the argument.

2.The purpose of analytical exposition is to present a balanced and well-reasoned argument about a specific topic or issue. It aims to persuade the reader or listener to accept the argument being presented.

3.The generic structure of analytical exposition consists of the following elements:

Introduction: Introduces the topic and presents the thesis statement, which outlines the main argument being made.Arguments: Presents a series of points that support the thesis statement. Each point is typically supported by evidence or examples.Rebuttal: Presents counterarguments and refutes them with evidence or examples.Conclusion: Summarizes the main points and restates the thesis statement, reinforcing the argument being made.

4.Some of the language features of analytical exposition include:

Use of logical connectors such as "therefore," "however," and "despite" to signal the relationships between ideas.Use of modal verbs such as "must," "should," and "could" to express degrees of certainty or obligation.Use of rhetorical questions to engage the reader or listener and to make a point.Use of statistical data, expert opinions, and examples to support the argument being made.

For example:

"According to a recent study, the average person spends over five hours a day on their phone. This means that the average person spends over 1,800 hours a year on their phone. Is this really the best use of our time? We must consider the negative effects of excessive phone usage, such as decreased productivity and social isolation. Therefore, we should make an effort to reduce our phone usage and find more meaningful ways to spend our time."
