10. Why is the Morning Sky the Same as the Night Sky?​


Jawaban:

Answer:

This is of course what is meant by the view of the sky. Basically the sky can be said to be black, not blue whether it is day or night. This is because space is dark and colorless. Dark space, because space (space) is just a sight, not absorbing traffic, so it's dark, you can't see anything (empty).

Because sunlight is scattered by aerosol particles (colloids) in the atmosphere, then to us on the earth's surface, the sky will generally appear blue. This spreading event is known as Rayleigh scattering.

Likewise, at night it will be blue, because the light received by the earth is also the same sunlight, the result of the moon's heat. Because these rays are the same, then the sky should be blue at night. And indeed the night sky is blue. This can be proven in conditions when there are no distracting light sources (low light pollution), in which the sky will look bluish.

Even though the night sky is also blue, we cannot see it due to the low light level. This is more because it limits our eye's ability to see color. The result we see and perceive the night sky is black.
